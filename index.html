Household Screening Step 1: Get oriented

Answer a few questions to screen households and build an action plan.

1. Which utilities does this home pay?

Check all investor-owned utilities (IOUs) that send bills to this address. If they mention a PUD or co-op, those are outside this tool.

2. Income snapshot

These bill discounts normally use about 60% of State Median Income as the main cut-off. If the household already qualifies for other low-income programs, that's a strong signal.

Community-Minded Coaching

  • Lead with relief, not paperwork. "There's a monthly discount you may qualify for on your utility bill. It's quick, based on your income, and you don't have to upload documents to apply."
  • Names & trust: Ask who should be listed on the bill and who feels safest being the contact. Explain that the utility decides eligibility, not your CBO.
  • PII & immigration anxiety: These applications generally don't ask about immigration status and often accept self-attested income. Review the form together on screen and let the customer skip anything they're not comfortable answering.
  • Stack funding on purpose. Make it explicit: the discount stacks with LIHEAP, OEAP, weatherization, and other aid. You're not "using up" their eligibility.
  • Normalize re-enrollment. Several programs require reconfirming income after a term (often 1-2 years). Capture a reminder in your CRM or calendar.

⚠️ Avoid promising confidentiality you can't guarantee. Instead: "The program has its own privacy rules. We can look at those together, and you can decide what feels right."
